Oscar François de Jarjayes

Oscar François de Jarjayes is the sixth and youngest daughter of General Jarjayes. Lacking a male heir, the General decides to raise his last daughter as a son and to name her Oscar. At the age of fourteen, Oscar becomes responsible for the safety of the new Queen of France, Marie Antoinette. During her years as a Royal Guard, Oscar gradually comes to understand the plight of France's poor and eventually decides to support their cause.

Oscar's characteristics

  • Birthday: 25/12/1755
  • Social class: Nobility
  • Military rank: Captain (later Commander) of the Royal Guards
  • Skills: Fencing and shooting

Oscar's friends and allies

  • André Grandier, Oscar's attendant and childhood friend
  • Marie Antoinette, Queen of France
  • Rosalie Lamorlière, a Parisian girl taken in the Jarjayes househould
  • Count Hans Axel von Fersen, a Swedish nobleman
